BlackJack, or 21, is a common casino game which involves dealing with probabilities. The purpose of the game is to have a greater card sum than the dealer without going over 21. The card sum, or count, works by adding your card values together, where the number cards refer to its number, i.e. a card that is a 6 would add 6 to your count, and face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are all worth 10, and the ace is valued at either 1 or 11, depending on what your count is. Everyone receives 2 cards and reveals their hand, while the dealer keeps one of their cards concealed, and players can either receive another card and hope they do not go over 21 and “bust”, or stay and hope that the dealer has a lower hand or that they “bust”. The dealer, on the other hand, has a more fixated structure of playing: if they have a count of 16 or less, they must take an additional card, and if the dealer has a count of 17 or over, they must stay. The dealer continues with their specific deck that is unshuffled until they run out of cards, where they shuffle the deck and start the dealing process once again.

One thing people do to supplement their betting patterns is “counting cards”. Essentially what it is doing is keeping track of the amount of cards that are high-value and low-value to better understand the likelihood of busting when the next card is dealt. Counting cards is difficult because it requires mentally keeping track of counts of several different categories of cards and counting cards only works if the player starts counting when the dealer first deals the shuffled deck. In other words, a player cannot begin counting cards when half of the cards in the current deck have already been played, it will be impossible to get an accurate count of the cards that have already been played.
